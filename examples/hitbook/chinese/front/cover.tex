\begin{cabstract}
随着人脸识别系统在生物特征提取、身份认证、安防监控等关键领域的广泛部署，其特征模板与模型输出所蕴含的敏感信息面临日益严峻的隐私泄露风险。攻击者可从泄露的特征向量模板或模型输出的类别标签中恢复用户面部图像，对个人隐私构成严重威胁。本文从模板重建人脸角度和标签重建人脸角度，分别提出高效方法，系统开展了人脸识别模型信息输出层面隐私脆弱性的研究。

针对以特征向量模板形式泄露信息的人脸识别模型，本研究提出基于角度约束的模板反演攻击方法（Angular-constraint Template Inversion Attack, ArcTIA）。本方法以角度约束对比学习损失为核心，通过负样本对比机制显式拉开生成特征与非目标类别的角度距离，使优化方向与识别器决策边界精准对齐。同时引入任务不确定性加权框架，以可学习参数自动调节像素重建与特征匹配的权重比例，并通过类内多样性约束防止模式崩塌。推理阶段利用模板条件梯度引导机制动态校正生成轨迹，实现对目标特征的精确逆向映射。ArcTIA在MOBIO数据集上于误识率$10^{-2}$时达到97.38\%的重建成功率，平均重建成功率87.54\%，在LFW数据集上FID指标降至18.27，重建图像的视觉效果显著提升。

针对以类别标签形式泄露信息的人脸识别模型，本研究提出基于人脸替换的模型反演攻击方法（Swapping Prior Model Inversion Attack，SwapMIA）。本方法以预训练扩散式人脸替换模型为先验模型，设计标签条件嵌入层将离散类别标签映射为连续身份嵌入向量，并采用低秩适配微调进行参数高效微调，大幅缩短训练时间与计算开销。在优化目标上，构建涵盖扩散先验保真度、分类引导有效性、感知质量等五维度的多目标损失框架，并通过渐进式三阶段训练策略实现从图像条件到纯标签条件的平滑迁移。SwapMIA在ArcFace分类器上目标准确率与评估准确率分别达到94.87\%与83.15\%，较基线方法均有显著提升，FID指标降至23.26，重建图像的视觉真实性明显改善。

本文研究通过在人脸识别模型的特征嵌入层面和分类输出层面，分别研究了从特征向量模板和类别标签重建人脸图像，测试了重建人脸图像攻击的隐私风险，对评估模型隐私脆弱性与设计针对性防护机制具有重要意义。
\end{cabstract}

\begin{eabstract}
With the widespread deployment of face recognition systems in biometric feature extraction, identity authentication, surveillance, and other critical domains, the sensitive information embedded in feature templates and model outputs faces growing privacy leakage risks. Through inverse reconstruction, facial images can be recovered from leaked feature vector templates or class labels output by models, posing serious threats to personal privacy. This research proposes efficient methods for these two inverse reconstruction scenarios respectively, to systematically reveal the privacy vulnerabilities of face recognition models at the information output level.

For face recognition models that output feature embedding vectors, this research proposes Angular-constraint Template Inversion Attack (ArcTIA), a method for inverse face image reconstruction from feature templates based on angle-constrained contrastive learning. The method centers on an angle-constrained contrastive learning loss that explicitly enlarges the angular distance between generated features and non-target classes through negative sample contrast, precisely aligning the optimization direction with recognizer decision boundaries. A task uncertainty weighting framework is also introduced to automatically adjust the balance between pixel reconstruction and feature matching losses via learnable parameters, and intra-class diversity constraints further prevent mode collapse. During inference, template-conditioned gradient guidance dynamically corrects the generation trajectory, enabling precise inverse mapping of target features. ArcTIA achieves a 97.38\% reconstruction success rate on MOBIO at False Match Rate =$10^{-2}$, with an average reconstruction success rate of 87.54\% and a FID of 18.27 on LFW.

For face recognition models that leak information in the form of class labels, this research proposes Swapping Prior Model Inversion Attack (SwapMIA), a method for inverse face image reconstruction from class labels based on face swapping model prior transfer. The method adopts a pretrained diffusion-based face swapping model as the generative backbone, with a label-conditioned embedding layer mapping discrete class labels to continuous identity embedding vectors, and employs Low-Rank Adaptation for parameter-efficient fine-tuning, greatly reducing training time and computational overhead. A multi-objective loss framework covering five dimensions---including diffusion prior fidelity, classification guidance effectiveness, and perceptual quality---is constructed, and a progressive three-stage training strategy achieves smooth transition from image-conditioned to pure label-conditioned generation. SwapMIA achieves 94.87\% target accuracy and 83.15\% evaluation accuracy on ArcFace, both significantly outperforming baseline methods, with FID reduced to 23.26 and noticeably improved visual realism of reconstructed images.

The above research systematically reveals the inverse reconstruction privacy risks of face recognition models at the feature embedding and confidence probability output levels, providing important reference for evaluating model privacy vulnerabilities and designing targeted defense mechanisms.
\end{eabstract}
